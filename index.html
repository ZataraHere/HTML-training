This poem uses the dialect of the Caribbean, with its abbreviated grammar and characteristic rhythms. Limbo is not only the title of this poem, but also a lively dance in which the dancers use athleticism and muscular strength to pass under a horizontal stick, each dancer challenging the next to go lower. In this poem the limbo stick is a metaphor for slavery;
for the ship in which the future slaves travelled, for the whip that punishes them. The title Limbo is a pun. As well as a dance, limbo it is another word for purgatory, the state of existing on the edge of hell, suffering penance before being admitted to heaven.

This part of the Catholic Christian doctrine applies appropriately to those who suffered as slaves. The poem is divided into uneven, short-lined stanzas. The is no rhyme scheme.
